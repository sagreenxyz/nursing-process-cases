# Head-to-Toe Physical Assessment

## Overview

**Head-to-Toe Assessment** is a systematic, organized physical examination proceeding from head to feet. This is the most fundamental nursing assessment skill and is used universally across all clinical settings.

**Why Head-to-Toe?**
- âœ“ **Systematic** - ensures nothing is missed
- âœ“ **Efficient** - organized workflow minimizes patient repositioning
- âœ“ **Universal** - understood across all healthcare disciplines
- âœ“ **Baseline** - establishes comprehensive baseline for comparison
- âœ“ **Adaptable** - can be abbreviated or expanded based on situation

**When to Use:**
- Admission assessments
- Beginning of shift assessments
- Post-procedure monitoring
- Any time comprehensive physical exam is needed

**Technique:** Listen systematically, anterior and posterior, comparing side-to-side. Use diaphragm of stethoscope.

**Breath Sounds:**
- **Quality:** Diminished bilaterally throughout (reduced air movement)

- **Inspiration:** Decreased volume

| Location | Sound | Timing | Clears with Cough? |
|----------|-------|--------|-------------------|
| Throughout all fields | Expiratory wheezes (high-pitched) | Expiration | No |
| Bilateral bases | Coarse crackles | Inspiration | Partial |

**Clinical Significance:** Wheezes indicate bronchospasm. Crackles indicate secretions and/or fluid (infection at right base). Diminished sounds indicate poor air movement - concerning finding.
