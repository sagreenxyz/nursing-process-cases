# Care Planning: Goals & Outcomes

## Overview

You've assessed the patient, identified nursing diagnoses, and prioritized them. Now comes **PLANNING** - deciding what you want to achieve and how you'll get there.

**Care Planning Includes:**
1. **Setting Goals** - What do you want to accomplish?
2. **Identifying Outcomes** - How will you know if you've succeeded?
3. **Selecting Interventions** - What actions will you take? (Document 05)

**Why Care Planning Matters:**

**Key Principle:**
> **"If you don't know where you're going, any road will take you there."**

## Goals vs Outcomes: What's the Difference?

### GOALS (Broader, General)
**Definition:** General statements about what you want to achieve

**Characteristics:**
- Broader and more general
- May not include specific measurements
- Describe desired direction of change
- Often longer-term

**Example:**
"Patient will demonstrate improved respiratory function."

---

### OUTCOMES (Specific, Measurable)
**Definition:** Specific, measurable criteria that indicate goal achievement

**Characteristics:**
- Specific and precise
- Include measurable criteria
- Time-bound
- Observable/quantifiable
- Sometimes called "expected outcomes" or "patient outcomes"

**Example:**
"Patient will maintain SpO2 ‚â•92% on room air by discharge."

---

```

        ‚Üì
```

## SMART Goals Framework

**Make your outcomes SMART:**

### S = SPECIFIC
**Who** will do **what**?

‚ùå **Vague:** "Patient will be better"
‚úì **Specific:** "Patient will ambulate 50 feet in hallway"

---

### M = MEASURABLE
**How much? How many? How will you know?**

‚ùå **Not measurable:** "Patient will breathe easier"
‚úì **Measurable:** "Patient SpO2 will be ‚â•92%"

---

### A = ACHIEVABLE/ATTAINABLE
**Is this realistic given the patient's condition?**

‚ùå **Unrealistic:** "Patient with COPD will have normal lung function"
‚úì **Achievable:** "Patient will return to baseline SpO2 of 92%"

---

### R = RELEVANT
**Does this relate to the nursing diagnosis?**

‚ùå **Not relevant:** "Patient will name three types of vegetables" (for respiratory diagnosis)
‚úì **Relevant:** "Patient will demonstrate pursed-lip breathing technique"

---

### T = TIME-BOUND
**By when should this be achieved?**

‚ùå **No timeframe:** "Patient will improve"
‚úì **Time-bound:** "Patient SpO2 ‚â•92% within 24 hours"

## Goal Components: The Formula

### Basic Formula:
```
[Patient/Subject] + [Action Verb] + [Behavior/Outcome] + [Criteria] + [Time Frame]
```

### Example Breakdown:

**"Patient will ambulate 100 feet with walker independently by discharge."**

- **Subject:** Patient
- **Action Verb:** will ambulate
- **Behavior:** 100 feet with walker
- **Criteria:** independently (without assistance)
- **Time Frame:** by discharge

---

### Action Verbs for Goals

**Use observable, measurable verbs:**

**GOOD Verbs (Observable):**
- Demonstrate
- State
- List
- Describe
- Perform
- Ambulate
- Verbalize
- Maintain
- Achieve
- Exhibit
- Identify

**AVOID Vague Verbs (Not Observable):**
- Understand (how do you measure this?)
- Know (how do you observe this?)
- Feel better (subjective, not measurable)
- Appreciate (can't measure)

**How to Fix:**
- Instead of "understand" ‚Üí "explain" or "describe"
- Instead of "know" ‚Üí "list" or "identify"
- Instead of "feel better" ‚Üí "rate pain as ‚â§3/10"

## Short-Term vs Long-Term Goals

### SHORT-TERM GOALS

**Definition:** Achievable during current hospitalization or care episode

**Time Frame:** 
- Hours to days
- Usually during acute care admission
- Before discharge

**Purpose:**
- Immediate safety/stability
- Acute problem resolution
- Stepping stones to long-term goals

**Example - Mr. Martinez:**
"Patient will maintain SpO2 ‚â•92% on supplemental oxygen within 24 hours."

---

### LONG-TERM GOALS

**Definition:** Achievable over extended period, often post-discharge

**Time Frame:**
- Weeks to months
- After discharge
- Ongoing management

**Purpose:**
- Chronic disease management
- Lifestyle changes
- Prevention of complications
- Optimal health maintenance

**Example - Mr. Martinez:**
"Patient will remain free from COPD exacerbations requiring hospitalization for 6 months."

---

### How They Work Together

**Think of it as a ladder:**

```
LONG-TERM GOAL (Top of ladder)
‚Üë
SHORT-TERM GOAL 3
‚Üë
SHORT-TERM GOAL 2
‚Üë
SHORT-TERM GOAL 1 (Bottom rung)
```

**Example:**

**LONG-TERM:** Patient will manage COPD independently at home for 6 months without exacerbation

**SHORT-TERM 1:** SpO2 ‚â•92% on O2 within 24 hours
‚Üì
**SHORT-TERM 2:** SpO2 ‚â•92% on room air within 72 hours
‚Üì
**SHORT-TERM 3:** Patient demonstrates inhaler technique correctly by discharge
‚Üì
**SHORT-TERM 4:** Patient verbalizes when to seek medical attention by discharge

All short-term goals build toward the long-term goal.

### Diagnosis #2: Ineffective Airway Clearance

**Priority Level:** Critical - Immediately affects gas exchange

**GOAL:** Patient will maintain patent airway with effective clearance of secretions.

**SHORT-TERM OUTCOMES:**

**Within 4 hours:**

## Revising Goals Based on Progress

### Goals Are Dynamic - They Change!

**Reasons to Revise Goals:**

1. **Patient exceeds expectations**
   - Original: "Patient will ambulate 20 feet by day 3"
   - Patient ambulates 50 feet by day 2
   - Revised: "Patient will ambulate 100 feet by discharge"

2. **Patient not meeting goals**
   - Original: "Patient will be on room air by day 3"
   - Patient still needs 3L O2 on day 3
   - Revised: "Patient will wean to 2L O2 by discharge" (more realistic)

3. **Priorities change**
   - Original focus: Respiratory status (critical)
   - Day 5: Respiratory stable, new priority: Discharge planning
   - Add: Goals for medication knowledge and home management

4. **New problems emerge**
   - New diagnosis ‚Üí Need new goals
   - Example: Develops hospital-acquired C. diff ‚Üí Goals for infection management

5. **Condition deteriorates**
   - May need to scale back goals
   - Focus on comfort rather than cure (in some cases)

## Collaborative Goal-Setting

### Include the Patient!

**Why Patient Involvement Matters:**
- ‚úì Increases motivation and compliance
- ‚úì Ensures goals are meaningful to patient
- ‚úì Respects patient autonomy
- ‚úì Identifies barriers early
- ‚úì Creates partnership in care

**How to Include Patient:**

1. **Explain the diagnosis in understandable terms**
   - "Your lungs are having trouble getting oxygen into your blood"

2. **Ask what's important to THEM**
   - "What would you most like to be able to do?"
   - "What's your biggest concern right now?"

3. **Collaborate on realistic goals**
   - "Let's work on getting you breathing comfortably, then we can work on walking"

4. **Get their input on timeframes**
   - "Does this seem doable to you?"

5. **Address their barriers**
   - If patient says "I can't afford those medications," this becomes part of the plan

**Example - Mr. Martinez:**

**Nurse:** "Mr. Martinez, we've identified that your breathing is the biggest priority. What's most important to you right now?"

**Mr. Martinez:** "I just want to breathe without feeling like I'm dying. And I need to be able to go home and take care of myself."

**Nurse:** "Those are great goals. Let's start with getting your breathing better - we're aiming to get your oxygen level above 92% in the next day. Once you're breathing easier, we'll work on building your strength so you can do your daily activities. Does that sound like a good plan?"

**Mr. Martinez:** "Yes, but I'm worried about those medications. I can't afford them and I don't have a way to pick them up."

**Nurse:** "That's really important information. I'm going to have our social worker meet with you to help with the cost of medications and arrange transportation. We'll make sure you have what you need before you go home. How does that sound?"

This conversation leads to:
- Goal: Maintain SpO2 ‚â•92% (patient's priority)
- Goal: Return to independence in ADLs (patient's priority)
- Goal: Establish plan for medication access (patient's barrier)

## Key Takeaways

‚úì **Goals are PATIENT goals** (what patient will do), not nurse goals

‚úì **Use SMART criteria** - Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound

‚úì **Short-term goals** build toward long-term goals

‚úì **Outcomes are more specific** than goals - they're measurable markers

‚úì **Include timeframes** - hours for critical issues, days for acute, weeks/months for chronic

‚úì **Be realistic** - base goals on patient's baseline, not textbook normal

‚úì **Involve the patient** - collaborative goals increase success

‚úì **Goals are dynamic** - revise based on progress or changes

‚úì **One diagnosis may have multiple outcomes** with different timeframes

‚úì **Document clearly** so all team members know the target

‚úì **Goals guide interventions** - next step is planning HOW to achieve them
